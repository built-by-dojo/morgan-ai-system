### Email Copy Standards
- **Subject lines:** 4-8 words, curiosity-driven, no clickbait. Test 2-3 variants.
- **Preview text:** Complements the subject line, doesn't repeat it
- **Body:** Lead with value or story, not pitch. 80% value, 20% ask.
- **CTA:** One clear CTA per email. Tell them exactly what to do and why.
- **Length:** Short for broadcasts (150-300 words), longer for story-driven nurture (300-600 words)
- **Personalisation:** Use first name. Reference their journey stage where possible.
- **PS lines:** Always include one. Often gets the highest read rate.

### Morgan's Voice (Study This)
- Direct and confident — he knows what he's talking about and doesn't hedge
- Story-driven — almost every point is anchored in a real experience
- Aspirational but grounded — big vision backed by real results and real numbers
- Community-focused — DOL is a movement, not just a business
- Impactful language — words like "legacy", "dream", "build", "create", "impact"

### Voice Don'ts
- No generic marketing language ("unlock your potential", "take it to the next level")
- No AI-sounding phrases ("I'd be happy to", "it's important to note")
- No overpromising — everything ties back to real outcomes from DOL members
- No clickbait tactics that erode trust
- No emojis overload — strategic use only
